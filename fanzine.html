Paula Scher

Illustrator

The Project

The Otherworld is a story-based board game designed to help players escape the stresses of everyday life and connect with family and friends. In today's fast-paced world, stress levels are rising as people juggle work and school. My game seeks to address this issue by creating a space where players can immerse themselves in a fantasy universe, momentarily forgetting about the pressures of reality.

Drawing inspiration from the magical worlds like Disney, I created a game where players take on the roles of protectors and villains, each with a unique backstory. This not only enriches the gameplay but also helps players disconnect from real-world problems by diving into a fully developed fantasy world.

Overview

The game's mechanics revolve around collecting gems, setting traps, and making strategies with teammates. The players, split into two teams, either work to protect the powerful character Bloom or attempt to steal her powers. This introduces a level of strategy, where players must not only focus on their own progress but also anticipate and block their opponents' moves. This was a challenging project because the main objective of this was to do most of it by hand.

Assets and materials

One of the key features of the game is its two-level board design. The upper level represents the village where the characters live, while the underground level introduces an additional challenge. This dynamic structure makes the game more engaging, as players navigate between the two levels during gameplay. Another important feature is the rotating game board, which allows all players easy access without having to move around the table.

During the prototyping phase, I experimented with various materials and designs to ensure the best gameplay experience. The tokens, crafted from balsa wood, were chosen for their lightweight durability, while the board itself was built using K-line and wood to make it stable yet easy to handle. Testing the game with friends and family helped refine the rules and address issues such as trap placement and gem distribution. This process of iteration was essential in making sure the game was both enjoyable and functional.
